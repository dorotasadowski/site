Tulle town hall's order


Carry a large canvas for a room takes place not only for weddings but also local councils and other meetings has been a great challenge for me. Soon, the idea of meeting and union but also strong emotions, disagreement came to my mind. A wish, that of providing a light, a dream and a calm in the work done.

On the canvas in the foreground, three trees, two trees together to face alone is the difference, the meeting of opposites but the magic moment of the Union. A shadow moves towards the couple, he is present with its shadows and soft light, hope, hand outstretched. Two fragments of shadows to the left of the couple is their record, their past. A red field in the distance, caressed by a light, points to the two trees entwined. He enriched the present. Is it love? Basically, the horizon symbolize the future, a yellow light escapes it. It invites the eye to see beyond. The colors are warm, protective like the sky or the earth.

I have called "appeasement" as the time of appeasement is a time we all seek when emotions are too strong. Marriage is it an answer to our loneliness? The day of the union, a time of sharing and fat? But the marriage would it not also the union of opposites with sources of conflict? Meetings and sources of conflicts of opposition? The appeasement would be a benefactor expected presence. A painting would it bring that presence? It is my hope.

Only trees in the landscape, men have they all gone? Of course not. But for me the tree is an expression of nature, he plays just like the man, "being of both worlds" with its roots planted in the earth and its branches directed towards the sky. These two worlds that are the spirit and matter come together to find the meaning, that our eyes want to give it.

 

2010

180 x 395 cm
